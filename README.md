<section id="intro">
  <h2>Introduction</h2>
  <p>
    Roadside waste has become a major challenge in rapidly growing urban areas. 
    With increasing population, urbanization, and consumerism, cities generate large volumes 
    of mixed solid waste every day. This project focuses on improving waste collection efficiency, 
    segregation, and community participation to create cleaner, smarter cities.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="scope">
  <h2>Scope</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Keeps roads and public areas clean.</li>
    <li>Reduces pollution and health risks.</li>
    <li>Supports recycling and reuse.</li>
    <li>Useful in cities, towns, and rural areas.</li>
    <li>Keeps the environment safe and sustainable.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="need">
  <h2>Need for Study</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Rapid urbanization increases roadside waste.</li>
    <li>Unmanaged waste leads to pollution and disease spread.</li>
    <li>Improper disposal reduces city aesthetics and tourism.</li>
    <li>There is a need for cost-effective, eco-friendly handling methods.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="aim">
  <h2>Aim & Objectives</h2>
  <p><strong>Aim:</strong> To develop an efficient and sustainable waste collection system using smart technologies.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Identify types and sources of waste.</li>
    <li>Design systems for segregation, collection, and recycling.</li>
    <li>Promote composting and waste-to-energy conversion.</li>
    <li>Encourage public participation and awareness.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="theory">
  <h2>Theory / Literature Review</h2>
  <p>
    Pune generates around 2,258 tonnes of waste daily, projected to rise to 3,600 tonnes by 2031. 
    Common issues include lack of segregation, poor collection, limited use of technology, 
    and financial constraints. To overcome these, cities must adopt smart monitoring systems, 
    IoT sensors, and data-driven models for efficient waste management.
  </p>
</section>

<section id="pune">
  <h2>Case Study: SWaCH & KKPKP Model (Pune)</h2>
  <p>
    The SWaCH cooperative, formed in 2008 under Pune Municipal Corporation, 
    employs over 3,000 waste pickers for door-to-door waste collection. 
    This decentralized model, based on inclusion and segregation, 
    has achieved 95% segregation in Pune — among the highest in India.  
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Involves informal sector workers for sustainable livelihoods.</li>
    <li>Reduces cost compared to centralized systems.</li>
    <li>Promotes circular waste economy.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="ai">
  <h2>AI-based Garbage Weight Estimation</h2>
  <p>
    The project utilizes <strong>AI techniques</strong> to calculate the approximate 
    weight of garbage piles using dimensions and standard density values.
  </p>
  <p><strong>Example 1:</strong> 4m × 2m × 0.5m → Volume = 4 m³ × 200 kg/m³ = <b>800 kg</b></p>
  <p><strong>Example 2:</strong> 5.5m × 1.5m × 0.5m → Volume = 4.125 m³ × 200 kg/m³ = <b>825 kg</b></p>
</section>

<section id="community">
  <h2>Community Engagement</h2>
  <p>
    Community involvement is crucial for the project’s success. 
    Awareness drives, door-to-door education, and segregation at source 
    ensure long-term sustainability. Before-and-after comparisons 
    show significant improvements in cleanliness and waste reduction.
  </p>
</section>
